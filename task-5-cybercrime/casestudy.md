What is Cyberbullying?

Cyberbullying is the use of digital platforms — social media, messaging apps, online
gaming, or email — to repeatedly harass, threaten, humiliate, or intimidate another
person. Unlike traditional bullying, it is not limited by physical location or time of day.
Riya, a 19-year-old first-year engineering student, shared a personal opinion in a
private college WhatsApp group about a topic she felt strongly about. One of the
members took a screenshot of her message and shared it without her consent in a
larger group of over 200 students.
The screenshot was shared with mocking captions added by classmates.
Other students forwarded it to their own networks and social media stories.
Riya began receiving hostile replies, insults, and threats from people she had never interacted with.
Anonymous accounts created on Instagram began posting edited screenshots designed to further humiliate her.
Riya started avoiding classes, withdrew from group chats, and developed severe anxiety within two week.
Students who express unpopular opinions, those perceived as "different," or anyone
who has had a conflict with a peer. Young women are disproportionately targeted
in image-based or reputation-based forms of cyberbullying.
